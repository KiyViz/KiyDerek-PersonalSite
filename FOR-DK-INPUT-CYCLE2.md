## Distribution Strategy (developed from review council gaps)

The CEO, CFO, and Marketing Director all agreed: **no traffic strategy = no business.** The site is a conversion layer. Something must feed it. Here's the plan.

### The Hormozi problem (Derek's own words)

You're not following "do one thing until $1M/yr." You have:

- derekkiy.com (generalist practice)
- kiyviz.com (infosplainers)
- justtheconstitution.org (civic project)
- museum-DTE (productized service idea)
- $97 consultations (new)
- elDelicious (gaming channel)

That's 6 things. Hormozi would say pick one. But you're not building a SaaS — you're building a creative practice. Creative practices are inherently multi-channel. **The fix isn't fewer things — it's one funnel that all things feed into.**

### The single funnel

```
ALL ACTIVITY
    ↓
derekkiy.com (trust layer)
    ↓
"Book a Call — $97" (conversion point)
    ↓
consultation (relationship + revenue)
    ↓
done-for-you project OR referral OR repeat consultation
```

Every piece of content, every project, every conversation points at derekkiy.com. derekkiy.com points at the Calendly button. The button converts. Everything else is distribution for this one funnel.

### Pick ONE distribution channel for 90 days

You said you're bad at networking and currently spending 0% on sales. The fix is ONE channel, ONE cadence, for 90 days. Not three channels. Not "I should also post on Instagram." One.

**Option A: LinkedIn (recommended)**

- Why: Your buyers (museum directors, foundation POs, campaign managers, NGO leads) are on LinkedIn. Your best work (NYT placement, trilingual IOA, CalChamber) is LinkedIn-credible content. You already have a profile.
- Cadence: 2 posts/week. Alternate between (1) a case study highlight with one image + 3-sentence insight, and (2) a short take on a topic in your domain (bilingual design, museum accessibility, policy communication).
- CTA on every post: "More at derekkiy.com" or "I help orgs like [X] do [Y] — link in bio."
- Time cost: ~2 hours/week (30 min per post, including image selection).
- 90-day goal: 50+ posts, measurable traffic to derekkiy.com, 5+ consultation bookings from LinkedIn-sourced visitors.

**Option B: Direct outreach (email)**

- Why: You have a network. IOA contacts, past clients, museum colleagues, McGill alumni, campaign world. These people already know you exist — they just don't know you're available.
- Cadence: 5 emails/week. Personalized, not templated. "Hey [name], I just launched my practice site — wanted to share what I've been working on. [link to specific case study relevant to them]. If you know anyone who needs [X], I'd love an intro."
- Time cost: ~2.5 hours/week (30 min per email including research).
- 90-day goal: 65 personalized outreach emails, 10+ replies, 3+ consultation bookings.

**Option C: Reddit/community (your natural habitat)**

- Why: You said you already give design feedback on Reddit for fun. Get paid for it instead. Find subreddits where people ask for design help (r/design_critiques, r/web_design, r/smallbusiness, r/freelance). Give genuinely useful advice. Link to derekkiy.com in your profile/flair. When appropriate: "I do paid consultations for exactly this — $97 for 20 minutes + a written action plan."
- Cadence: 30 min/day, 5 days/week. 2-3 substantive comments per session.
- Time cost: ~2.5 hours/week.
- 90-day goal: 200+ comments, measurable profile clicks, 5+ consultation bookings from Reddit.

**Option D: In-person networking (your stated sales script context)**

- Why: The elevator pitch exists for a reason. Local Escondido/San Diego design meetups, chamber events, museum openings, IOA events.
- Cadence: 1 event/week. Business card with derekkiy.com + QR code to Calendly.
- Time cost: ~3-4 hours/week (including travel).
- 90-day goal: 12+ events, 30+ new contacts, 3+ consultation bookings.

### The recommendation

**Start with Option A (LinkedIn) + Option B (direct outreach) combined.** They're complementary: LinkedIn builds public visibility, outreach converts existing relationships. Total time: ~4.5 hours/week. That's your "50% sales" allocation in practice.

If you can only pick ONE: **Option B (direct outreach).** It has the highest conversion rate because these people already trust you. The site gives them a reason to act.

### Distribution is the unlock

The $97 consultation, the portfolio, the CSYA scheme, the controlled-chaos hero — none of it matters without people seeing it. The site is the engine. Distribution is the fuel. Pick a channel, commit for 90 days, measure, adjust.
